Data Visualization

Our Data Visualization tools provide vital information to our customers to ensure optimum performance of their Industrial ecosystem. Some key areas where our Vis tools can be used are monitoring the health of assets for maintenance optimization, a success or failure of factory processes, or the operational status of a group of plants. Creating readable visualizations play a vital role in helping our customers make split second decisions on how to proceed with handling a variety of situations that in turn could save lives and reduce maintenance costs.

Data Vis Framework Charts

Parallel Coordinates Used for analyzing multivariate data to look for comparisons.
Pie Chart Standard pie chart showing values relative to each other in a circular format.
Polar Chart/ Radar Chart Both are similar to the parallel coordinates chart in the sense that they are used to view multivariate data. The difference is the presentation is in a circular format. Often times, these charts are preferred when sensors are displayed in a circular fashion on an asset or the element spins.
Spark A small line chart without axes or measures that provides a user a glimpse of a trend.
Timeseries provides means to analyze data over a period of time.
XY Chart Used to find correlations between two or more parameters or tags.

Simple Charts

Percent Circle Used for conveying a single value as a percentage of a whole circle.
Gauge A simple data visualization component for conveying a single value as a percentage of a circular arc.
Simple Area Chart Similar to the line chart with the area below the line filled in. Provides an easy way to quickly compare data.
Simple Bar Chart Provides a quick way to visualize data in single or stacked bars.
Simple Horizontal Bar Chart Provides a quick way to visualize data in single or stacked bars.
Simple Line Chart Used to visualize linear data in a simplified way for quick assessment.
Simple Win-Loss Chart Visualizes a series of positive & negative values as a bar chart.

General Guidelines

Axis


Timeseries / Parallel /Bar

X-Axis
Do Do center label values on the axis line and et a vertical orientation of the axis label.
Don't Don't offset label values or set a horizontal orientation of axis label.
Y-Axis
Do Do set label and values to a horizontal orientation with the axis.
Don't Don't offset label values or set a horizontal orientation of axis label.
Axis Lines
Do Do keep axis lines light to make the data stand out and increase scanability.
Don't Don't make axis lines dark or allow axis lines to float next to values.

Radar & Polar

Do Keep values centered on the axis line and in containers to allow for optimum readability. Labels should sit above or next to the axis line.
Don't Don't place values directly on the axis line without a container box or display labels inside the chart.

Color Treatment
